J. K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling, born Joanne Rowling on July 31, 1965,
is a British author best known for creating the immensely popular "Harry Potter" series.
Before achieving fame as a writer, Rowling worked various jobs and struggled financially as a single mother. However,
her life changed dramatically with the publication of "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone"
(titled "Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone" in the United States) in 1997.
